# Our approach

Play therapy creates a **safe, predictable relationship** where a child can express feelings, try new ways of coping, and feel seen.

This section is placeholder content. You may wish to describe your theoretical orientation (e.g. child-centered, filial, trauma-informed) in your own words.

## What play therapy can support (placeholder)

Children and families often seek play therapy when working through:

- Big feelings, worry, or low mood
- Changes at home or school
- Difficulty with peers or family relationships
- Processing difficult experiences in a developmentally appropriate way

We move at the child’s pace and keep caregivers informed in ways that honor privacy and your therapeutic goals.
